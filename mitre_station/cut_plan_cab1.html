Cabinet 1 – Plywood Cut Plan

Carcass only  ·  ¾" plywood  ·  1 sheet 4×8 (48" × 96")

Sheet 1 of 1  ·  48" × 96"

Scale: 5 px = 1"  ·  Dashed red = saw cuts
⅛" kerf accounted for throughout  ·  Grain runs along 96" axis (vertical)

Cut List — 5 pieces from 1 sheet

Label Part Cut Size Qty
A Side 23½" × 30³⁄₁₆" 2
B Bottom 39⁷⁄₈" × 23½" 1
C Nailer (top back) 39⁷⁄₈" × 4" 1
D Centre divider 23½" × 29⁷⁄₁₆" 1

Sheet utilization: ~70%
Main offcut: 19⅜" × 56" — reserve for drawer fronts

Cut Sequence

  1. Rip 1 — full sheet at 23½" from left edge
    → Strip 1 (23½" × 96") + 24⅜" remainder
  2. Rip 2 — remainder at 23½"
    → Strip 2 (23½" × 96") + ¾" edge scrap
  3. Strip 1 — cross-cuts along 96" length:
    30³⁄₁₆" → A1 (Side)  [⅛" kerf]
    30³⁄₁₆" → A2 (Side)  [⅛" kerf]
    29⁷⁄₁₆" → D (Divider)
    End scrap: 5¹⁵⁄₁₆" × 23½"
  4. Strip 2 — cross-cut at 39⁷⁄₈" → B (Bottom)
    Remaining: 56" × 23½" panel
  5. 56" × 23½" remainder:
    Rip at 4" from edge → 4" × 56" sub-strip  [⅛" kerf]
    Cross-cut sub-strip at 39⁷⁄₈" → C (Nailer)
    Sub-strip scrap: 4" × 16"  ·  Large offcut: 19⅜" × 56"

Tip: complete all full-sheet rips first, then cross-cuts, then sub-rips.
